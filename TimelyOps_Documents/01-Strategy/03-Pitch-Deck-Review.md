## 🟡 Content Gaps — Consider Adding

### Slide 2 (The Problem) — Missing the Real Pain
The slide lists three problems (scattered tools, per-user pricing, feature gates) but doesn't hit the emotional core. The Product Briefing has stronger language: *"TimelyOps competes with the owner's own memory — not with generic field service software."* That's a sharper hook.

Suggested addition to problem slide:
> "You're the system. Everything lives in your head. If you're sick, the business stops."

### Slide 6 (How We Compare) — Slide Appears Empty
The comparison table slide has a heading but the actual table content didn't render in the text extraction — it may be an image or the data is missing. Verify this slide has actual competitor comparison content before presenting.

### No Social Proof / Traction Slide
There's no slide showing that TimelyOps is real and live. Adding a simple traction slide between the plans and the CTA would significantly increase credibility:

> "Live at timelyops.com • Founding customer onboarded • 3 referrals in pipeline • Platform fully built and operational"

This is especially important if you're presenting to investors or partners.

---

## ✅ What's Working Well

**Slide 4 — The Connected Pipeline** is your best slide. The 6-step flow (Quote → Approve → Schedule → Complete → Collect → Invoice) makes the product concrete and tangible. This is more compelling than any feature list.

**Slide 8 — Worker View** is smart to include. It shows you've thought about the actual people using the app, not just the business owner. Keep it.

**Slide 9 — Getting Started** lowers perceived friction effectively. The 4-step onboarding flow ("we set up your account, we import your clients") is a real differentiator vs. DIY setup competitors.
