## Terms of Service — What Changed (Draft → Final)

**Length:** Draft = ~7,800 characters / Final = ~14,000 characters (nearly 2× longer)

### What Was Added in v2

| New Section | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **"End Client" definition** | Drafts didn't define end clients at all. Final adds a clear definition covering people who interact via the AI booking agent, quote pages, and invoice pages. This matters because those people are using your platform without being your direct customer. |
| **Section 5: Authorized Users & Staff Accounts** | Clarifies that you (the Account Owner) are responsible for everything your workers do in the app, and that you must remove their access when they leave. Important for multi-tenant security. |
| **Section 6: AI-Powered Features** | Entire new section covering the AI booking agent. Includes disclaimers that AI responses may be wrong, that you (not TimelyOps) are responsible for reviewing AI-created bookings, and that Anthropic processes conversation data. This is essential legal protection. |
| **Additional prohibited use clauses** | The final adds: no spam via AI agent, no circumventing rate limits, no processing data you don't have authority to collect. These weren't in the draft. |
| **Section 4.3 update** | Draft: "you're responsible for data accuracy." Final adds specifically: "including incorrect pricing generated by the pricing matrix or AI booking agent." This explicitly limits your liability if the AI quotes the wrong price. |

### What Stayed the Same
- The core structure (definitions, eligibility, data ownership, pricing, termination, governing law) is consistent between both versions.
- Netherlands governing law and Amsterdam jurisdiction are the same in both.
- The 30-day change notice for pricing is consistent.

## Privacy Policy — What Changed (Draft → Final)

**Length:** Draft = ~8,400 characters / Final = ~12,200 characters (~45% longer)

### What Was Added in v2

| New Section | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Section 2.2 update — Property Details** | Final adds "property details (bedrooms, bathrooms, access codes, pet information)" to the list of business data collected. The draft didn't mention this. It's important to disclose that you store access codes and alarm codes. |
| **Section 2.3: AI Agent Conversation Data** | Entirely new section. Discloses that end client phone numbers, full conversation transcripts, property details, and booking preferences are collected and stored when someone uses the AI booking agent. This is essential for GDPR compliance since end clients interact with the system without creating an account. |

### What Stayed the Same
- GDPR data controller/processor framing
- Technical data collection (IP, browser, device)
- Data retention and deletion policies
- Third-party processor list

## Are the Final Versions Complete?

The finals are well-written and cover the key legal surface areas for your current product. A few things to watch for as you grow:

**Short term (no action needed now):**
- The AI section in the ToS correctly references "Anthropic" as the third-party AI provider. If you ever switch AI providers, this needs updating.

**Medium term (before Stripe goes live):**
- The ToS Section 5 (Pricing & Payment) says fees are billed monthly or annually. Once Stripe is live and automated, you'll want to be more specific about billing dates, failed payment handling, and grace periods.

**Longer term:**
- Once you expand beyond the Netherlands / EU, you may need a US-specific addendum for CCPA (California) if you have California customers.
